Room 304

Room PC Sound

Turn on the power switch on the Furman on the rack.

Just below the Furman unit, make sure the power switch on the ART MX822 is turned on, and that the LEVEL knobs for both the MAC SSL and MASTER channels are turned up.

On the back of the cart, locate the SSL 2+ interface. Make sure the MONITOR MIX knob is turned fully to the right, and set the MONITOR LEVEL knob to at least the 9 o'clock position.

On the room PC, go to the toolbar in the top right corner and under Sound Settings, select SSL 2+ (It would be highlighted blue when selected).

Note: In case the SSL 2+ doesn't show up in the output settings, replug the USB-C cable on the box.

External Sound Source

Connect to the aux cable in the room, and then turn on the power switch on the Furman.

Just below the Furman unit, make sure the power switch on the ART MX822 is turned on, and that the LEVEL knobs for both the MAC SSL and MASTER channels are turned up.

On your device, make sure External Headphones is selected as the playback device.

Room PC Video

Turn on the TV using the remote in the room.

By default, the room PC should be projected on the monitor. In case it isn't, press Input on the remote and ensure HDMI 2 is selected.

On the computer, open system preferences (in the dock at the bottom of the screen), click on displays, then select the arrangement tab.

If you would like to mirror the displays (such that the screen on the cart and on the wall are the same) make sure the box is checked. If you would like to extend the displays (such that the two screens are separate), make sure the box is unchecked.

Note: The blue box with the white tab on the top represents the computer monitor while the other box represents the room's screen. You can move around this second box to determine where the second screen exists in relation to the computer monitor.

When done, use the remote to turn off the projector.

Turn the red switch "off" on the Furman box in the rack.

Laptop Video

Connect your laptop to the HDMI cable in the room.

When the TV is turned on, you should automatically see your laptop being projected onto it. In case it's not, click Input on the TV remote and ensure HDMI 1 is selected.

MacBook:

In order to change display settings, open system preferences (in the dock at the bottom of the screen), click on displays, then select the arrangement tab.

If you would like to mirror the displays make sure the box is checked. If you would like to extend the displays, make sure the box is unchecked.

Windows:

First ensure SAMSUNG is selected as the playback device in the sound settings (bottom left corner of the toolbar).

Click the Windows icon on the bottom left corner, open 'Settings' and Click on 'System'.

Click on 'Display' and scroll down to 'Multiple Displays' setting.

Windows (continued):

If you would like to mirror the displays select 'Duplicate these displays'.

If you would like to extend the displays, select 'Extend these displays'. You can adjust how both screens relate to each other in the 'Rearrange your displays' section.

When done, use the remote to turn off the TV.

Turn the red switch "off" on the Furman box in the rack.
